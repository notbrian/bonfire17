Bonfire

a 24-hour hackathon for high schoolers

aug. 4th - 5th Brampton City Hall

What is Bonfire?

Bonfire is a hackathon, an overnight event where people can code a project together. At Bonfire, you will have 24 hours to make your idea a reality. This year, we're aiming to have 100 attendees with an even gender ratio.

When is Bonfire?

Bonfire will start at 4pm on the 4th of August. It will conclude at 10pm the next day, the 5th.

What Should I Bring?

Your laptop, phone, chargers, student ID, a sleeping bag, extension cords. Food will be provided!

How much will it cost?

Absolutely nothing! Breakfast lunch and dinner will be provided free of cost, and snacks will be available throughout the day if you get hungry. There is no entrance fee either.

What if I never been to a hackathon?

No problem. Bonfire is as much about learning as it is about hacking. We'll have experienced mentors who can help groups who may not have attended a hackathon before, or are new to programming in general.

How will I find a team?

Although it is recommended you find a team beforehand, we will match all those who do not have a team with others who need a team. We will ensure no one is left behind!

What can I win?

There will be different prizes for different categories and they will be announced at a later date.

Will I need to bring my own hardware?

If you have hardware you're looking to use, bring it with you! We will have basic hardware such as arduinos and sensors. If you have any specific requests, contact us at batman@projectcipher.io

My question isn't here!

If you have any questions, don't heisitate to shoot us an email at batman@projectcipher.io!
